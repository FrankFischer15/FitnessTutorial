HELPFUL FITNESS TIPS FOR STAYING ACTIVE THROUGH OUT THE DAY

  • HEALTHY EATING: Nutritional Basics

    • Ask almost any personal trainer and they'll tell you that regardless of your training goals, healthy eating is the backbone. Food is what fuels your body to reach your goals and without proper nutrition through quality foods, you're likely to stall. Maintain a balanced diet consisting of fruits, vegetables, carbohydrates, and proteins.

  • Prepare Ahead

    • Regardless of what you're doing, preparing yourself in advance gives you the best chance to accomplish your goals. Your approach to nutrition should be no different. It's best to prepare for your "upcoming day by prepping all your food in advance." It will ensure you do not eat bad foods or skip meals.

  • Eat more

    • Eating only three daily meals? Half the people aren't losing weight because they don't eat enough. Eat five times a day, about every three hours to stimulate your metabolism, including two mini meals between their three basic meals. With activity levels decreasing throughout the day, eat less as the day goes on.

  • Portion Control

    • You'll be eating more often, so paying attention to portions is extremely important. You need to make sure your chicken breasts, and meats, are no larger than your palm an that pastas, are no larger than your fists. Use smaller bowls, plates, and cups because studies show people serve themselves 20 to 40% more food when they're using larger plates.

  • MASS BUILDING: Muscle-Building Basics

    • Increase your caloric and complete protein intake. Then, when you enter the gym, focus on your form. Perform compound movements and train with weights on average around four times a week. Never underestimate the importance of rest. Remember, muscle tissue grows outside of the gym when you're giving your body time to relax and recover following your workouts.

  • Range of Motion

    • Don't take any short cuts. You should aim for the largest ROM (Range of Motion) you can achieve in your exercises. Why? Because your musles will do more work per rep, and it will result in your breaking down more tissue by the end of the workout.

  • Contradicting Cardio

    • Do you love your cardio exercises? During times of mass building you do not want to do large amounts of cardio. Why? Because chances are you'll be burning far too many calories. But if you still want to get in some cardio, a light jog a few days per week for 20 minutes is adequate.

  • ENDURANCE TRAINING: Perpare Yourself

    • When it comes to training for endurance, you'll need to be hydrated and be sure you're eating proplerly because, by its very nature, this form of training is very demanding on your body. You should be doing a good mix of cardio and weight training. And, to increase your aerobic capacity, you should incorporate intense interval training. You'll likely be sweating buckets and burning calories galore, so be prepared.

  • Heart Rate Monitor

    • Go out and buy a heart rate monitor, if you don't have one learn how to do it yourself. Monitor your heart rate and make sure your doing it for 60 minutes. Ask yourself am I working hard enough for 60 minutes? You're heart rate will tell you if you are or not.

  • Exhaust for Endurance

    • To further your endurance training, you need to put in total effort. So remember to fully exhaust the muscles.How can you do that? Get good at the bodyweight staples - pull-ups, chin-ups, push-ups, inverted rows, and squats. If you can master these movements for high reps, your muscles will get well conditioned.

  • Reduce Rest Time

    • It's always tempting to take a break when training, you should stick with rest times of 30 to 45 seconds between sets because it will help increase your overall endurance. If you are strength training, lift moderate to heavy weight and keep your rep range between 8 to 15 reps, or in you're running, mix duration cardio with sprinting.

  • Fight Fatigue

    • Fatigue may be your biggest enemy when endurance training. First Tip is to drink beet juice because "it can actually increase stamina by up to 16% and it helps your muscles produce more energy, more efficiently, making exercise less exhausting. Another way to boost your perdormance is by carefully selecting your music when people listen to favorable music their blood vessels expanded 26%.

  • STRENGTH TRAINING: Strengthening Basics

    • If you want to build strength, you have to set goals and be patient. It's important to be consistent and stick with your plan. When you're in the gym, don't get distracted. Stay focused on the task at hand. When you leave the gym, make sure you get proper rest and keep track of your progress. If you stay determined, your goals can be accomplished.

  • Motivation

    • Motivation is key. Count down when performing reps and look are your dominant hand while you're pushing up. It automatically includes a positive reinforcement because the dominant hand more easily and quickly moves the weight. Take cold showers or even ice baths to help replenish muscles.

  • Form Matters

    • When strength training, you'll be putting your body through very strenuous activity, so it's important to maintain proper form. Maintaining proper form you're guaranteed to activate the muscle groups that you are looking to train and, most importantly, you'll stay healthy and injury-free. The male/female who can stay healthiest can train the most and in the long run makes the most progress.

  • The Little Things

    • When you're strength training you have to pay attention to the little things because you're only as strong as your weakest link. If you notice a deficiency, address it in conjunction with your program.

  • Change Helps

    • If you want to make progress, somtimes you have to change things up. Why? It's important because you have to ensure that your body never gets adapted to what is coming next. Once that happens, you may notice diminishing strength gain results. You could switch up how heavy you're lifting, your tempo of an exercise, your rep/set count, or what time of the day you're lifting.

  • FAT LOSS: Fat Loss Basics

    • Forget calorie counting and concern yourself with body fat percentages. You must also start eating the right types of foods, which for most people will mean only lean meats, increasing fruit and vegetable intake, and removing any processed foods or those high in sugar. With your nutrition in check, the next step is performing intense interval cardio training, as well as weight training. And, don't forget to keep track of it all in a journal.

  • Photographic Evidence

    • Take pictures on a weekly basis. It's important to take "front, back, and side pictures all from the same angle, same lighting, same clothing because the scales lie, pictures do not, and you'll really be able to see a chance that way.

  • The Carb Factor

    • Learn to control your carbohydrate intake. They can cause your body to store fat. It's important to train cardiovascularly for more than 20 minutes at a time because in the first 20 minutes your body is basically burning carbohydrates for energy. With every continuous minute past the 20-minute mark, you'll begin burning more and more of the stored body fat.

  • Attacking the midsection

    • To flatten your waist you must go below your navel. People burn more calories the day after they did a lower body resistance training exercise than after they worked their upper bodies because the leg carries, for one thing, more mass. Getting a six-pack doesn't mean doing 100 crunches a day, it means proper diet.

  • Hydration To The Rescue

    • An often-overlooked factor, is to make sure that your GI tract is healthy, because that's how you absorb all your nutrients. Do so by consuming vitamins, fiber, minerals, a probiotic, and water. We suggest you drink ice cold water first thing in the morning adding you'll naturally boost your metabolism by up to 24% for 90 minutes. Also drink at least one gallon of water per day.
